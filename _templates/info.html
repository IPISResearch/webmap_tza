Interactive map of artisanal and small-scale mining and processing sites in northwest Tanzania

Overview

The present map is an interactive webmap of artisanal and small-scale mining and processing sites in 4 regions of northwest Tanzania, namely Geita, Shinyanga, Mara and Kigoma. For more data and contextual analysis, please read the accompanying report.

How to use this map

In the legend on the right, you can select the data set that you wish to make visible on the map. You can create your own custom maps by switching layers in the legend on or off to overlay them. Within layers, you can apply specific filters.

You can easily save or share any of your custom maps by copying the unique URL address.

Credits

This webmap is part of the project ‘Mapping the socio-economic and human rights impact of small and large-scale mining in northwest Tanzania’. This project is funded by the Belgian development cooperation as part of a program on Human Rights and Digitalisation.

Sources

The mining data on this map was collected by IPIS. The protected areas are part of the WRI Open Data. The geological data is from the Tanzania Geological and Mineral Information System. The baselayers are from Mapbox (using OSM data © OpenStreetMap contributors) and Bing.

The mining data behind the map is publicly available and can be downloaded on IPIS' Open Data page.
